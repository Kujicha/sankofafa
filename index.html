Educational Info

Importance of a Will

A will is a legal document that outlines your wishes for the distribution of your assets after your death. It ensures that your estate is handled according to your wishes and can help avoid disputes among family members.

What is a Trust?

A trust is a legal arrangement where one party (the grantor or settlor) transfers assets to another party (the trustee) to manage for the benefit of a third party (the beneficiary). It's a way to hold and manage assets for the benefit of others, often with specific instructions on how and when those assets are distributed.

What is an Executor?

An executor is a person appointed to carry out the instructions in your will. They are responsible for managing your estate, paying debts, and distributing assets to beneficiaries.

What is a Power of Attorney?

A power of attorney is a legal document that grants someone the authority to make decisions on your behalf if you become unable to do so. It can cover financial, medical, and personal matters.
